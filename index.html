I was born November 12th, 1986 - a drug addicted baby - resulting in a diagnosis of Cerebral Palsy. After surviving the neonatal intensive care unit, I underwent two life changing surgeries. One was on my spine, called a rhizotomy and the other was being shunted to relieve swelling on the brain, known as hydrocephalus. I was adopted as an infant, after having beaten tremendous odds and all the doctors counting me out.

I have had to overcome a physical disability, an eating disorder and being ostracised in school. Regardless, I have excelled as a student, a daughter, and friend, and volunteered overseas, realizing my greatest dream. I believe my challenges have instilled in me a particular tenacity and compassion that I bring to everything I will ever do.

All the education and Job experience I have I intend to channel into an example, an opportunity for excellence. I want to live my life as an advocate and champion for children with disabilities, as someone who stands up for anyone who believes they have been counted out. Finally, I long to be the best human being I can. I will dedicate myself fully to acting with integrity and commitment to be of service and use in the world. For, that is the purpose of any human life.
